3. Did EBP/CDEP services impact clinical and functional outcomes for clients who participated?

3.12 To what extent did parents/caregivers report a reduction in parent-related stress after receiving the intervention?

Definition: PSS item(s) measuring reduction in parent/caregiver-related stress after the intervention. Assessments showing improvement from pre- to post-time point for clients who discharged from an intervention during the designated time frame.

Scoring: If follow-up is LOWER than initial, that indicates a positive outcome. To calculate a total PSS score, items 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8, 17, and 18 should be reverse scored as follows: (1=5) (2=4) (3=3) (4=2) (5=1). The PSS score is then obtained by summing across all items. Higher scores indicate higher levels of perceived stress. If follow-up is LOWER than initial, that indicates a positive outcome.

  • 1 = Strongly disagree

  • 2 = Disagree

  • 3 = Undecided

  • 4 = Agree

  • 5 = Strongly Agree

Assumptions: Filtered out dates before 2024-01-01 and after the date the code was run. Filtered data that’s >3 days from initial assessment and paired to the LAST follow-up. Filtered positive outcomes that had NAs before computing %. Total score = NA if 2+ items are missing; used LAST follow-up if 2+ assessments exist; filtered out NA’s from follow-up date and positive outcome variable before calculating %.

Range: 18 - 90

Inclusion Criteria: 1. must have initial time point data (i.e., be enrolled in an intervention); 2. must have a valid assessment collected at both initial and discharge comparison time points

Final analysis: Regression - outcome = binary (4/5 vs other), predictors as for 3.01
